A consumer sentiment analysis and campaign strategy for EADEM's Le Chouchou Lip Softening Balm — and a study in using AI as a research foundation while overriding it with primary research.

---

## The Brand

EADEM is a fairly new name that has only recently entered the social media IT girl's zeitgeist. It positions itself as a modern, culturally forward, and artistically original company. On its website you will find calls that appeal to a multi-cultural audience: products named after international food delicacies, models that represent New York IT girls, and a board titled "Topic of Conversation" — a collection of Substack articles discussing immigrant experiences with self-care and identity, with EADEM products strategically embedded via hyperlinks.

## The Product

Le Chouchou Lip Softening Balm became an instant hit on TikTok beauty sub-cultures, recommended by niche influencers in their "My 10 Essentials" rundowns. Its utilitarian silver exterior and AI-esque, almost-industrial design create an illusion of artistry in makeup. It sells for $24.00 per piece.

## The Gap: Where the AI Started

Using Claude to run an initial analysis, it concluded: *"the product isn't failing, the marketing frame is."* The AI's hypothesis was that audiences are confused about what the product is — it's marketed as a balm, a gloss, a tint, AND a treatment — and that this category confusion was the core issue.

## My Counter-Thesis

Instead of a framing mistake, I believe the confusion is a **strategic choice.** A $24 lip gloss must be extraordinary to earn attention in a saturated market. Per McKinsey's State of Beauty 2025, lip balms rank among the categories consumers are least willing to splurge on — and products that earn a splurge are those with "meaningful performance differentiation." The gloss/tint ambiguity converts to curiosity, which drives traffic. Conversion then depends on **Consumer Perceived Value (CPV)**: once a buyer discovers it's a comprehensive treatment at a single price point, they convert.

## What the Data Actually Showed

I conducted manual sentiment research across Sephora, TikTok, and Reddit. The AI's "gloss vs. tint confusion" hypothesis **could not be corroborated on any platform.** Instead, the dominant sentiment was adverse physical reaction:

| Source | Sentiment | Scale |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Sephora | Dryness and irritation after use | 200+ negative reviews on adverse effects |
| Sephora | Sticky, cheap texture | 100+ negative reviews on value for money |
| Sephora | "Thought it was a gloss, it's a tint" | No significant reviews with this sentiment |
| TikTok | Lips flaky and dehydrated | 8–10 videos, 50k+ likes, 100+ agreeing comments |
| Reddit | Exfoliates and peels skin | 12 negative responses to thread |

## The Real Root Cause

The issue isn't a marketing mishap. Le Chouchou markets itself as a "Lip Softening Balm," failing to highlight its **exfoliating properties**, which dry out the skin barrier and increase adverse symptoms for consumers with sensitive and dry skin. It's both a hydrator and an exfoliant, creating an awkward application logic — exfoliants shouldn't be used during the day (UV vulnerability), which makes the tint factor irrelevant at night. The directions of use are unclear, so users reapply throughout the day assuming it works like a normal gloss.

## The Solution

EADEM needs to know its target audience and leverage its marketing to reach them. A campaign geared toward lip exfoliants — aimed at consumers seeking a 2-in-1 hybrid — would set correct expectations. Rather than masking the exfoliant properties, EADEM can highlight why the product is great for a **night out**, intuitively combining all functions while ensuring no new consumer is blindsided.

## Ad Copy: AI-Generated vs. My Judgment

Claude generated three variants based on my solution. I evaluated each:

| AI Output | My Decision | Reasoning |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| A — Occasion-led | Keep + Edit | Language sounds superficial and generated, off from EADEM's straightforward voice. Needs less gimmick, more action. |
| B — Transparency | Keep + Edit | Strong combination of explanation + how-to. Needs to feel more relevant, niche, and genuine. |
| C — Ritual/guidance | **Cut** | Reviews show the peel-off effect while sleeping isn't an ideal ritual. Users are better off layering hydrating products during a night out to avoid dryness. |

## Key Takeaway

Using AI as a research foundation saves time — but human experience, concerns, and cultural relevance are lost to AI analysis. This is where I leveraged my own knowledge and understanding of interrelated social phenomena to reach a beneficial strategy. Closing the gap between what consumers expect and what they get means the product must retain its CPV, achieved by using marketing strategies intuitively alongside rising trend cycles and audience interests.
